  - q: "What is the difference between lifespan and healthspan?"
    a: "Lifespan is the total years a person lives. Healthspan is the years lived in good physical and cognitive health. Many longevity strategies focus on extending healthspan rather than simply adding years."
  - q: "Does aging biology differ between people?"
    a: "Yes. Genetic predisposition, early-life exposures, lifestyle choices, and environment all influence how aging biology unfolds — which is why biological age can differ significantly from chronological age."
  - q: "Can lifestyle changes slow aging?"
    a: "Lifestyle factors such as regular exercise, sleep quality, and managing cardiometabolic risk can reduce disease risk and help preserve function. No proven therapy stops aging, but the evidence for lifestyle impact on healthspan is strong."

## Intro

This guide serves as a foundation for the **Aging & Longevity** topic hub. It explains the core ideas behind aging biology, lifespan, and healthspan, and links to deeper guides covering specific aspects of aging and preventive health.

Aging research focuses not only on how long people live, but also on **how well they live during those years**. Many modern discussions about longevity focus on extending **healthspan**—the years lived in good physical and cognitive health.

Understanding the basics of aging biology helps clarify which interventions are well supported by evidence and which claims remain speculative.

## Core Topics in Aging and Longevity

Aging research covers many different biological systems and health risks that change over time. The Aging & Longevity hub connects several key areas of evidence-based health and prevention.

Major topics explored in this section include:

- **[Skin aging and protection](/guides/skin-aging)**, including sunscreen, retinoids, vitamin C, and collagen support.
- **Cardiometabolic health**, including blood pressure, cholesterol, and [cardiovascular risk assessment](/guides/cardiovascular-risk-assessment).
- **Brain health and cognitive aging**, including dementia risk and neurological health — see the [Brain Health Hub](/guides/brain-health-hub).
- **Musculoskeletal aging**, including bone density, muscle loss, and fall risk.
- **Lifestyle and preventive health**, including nutrition, sleep, and physical activity.

Lifestyle factors have a strong influence on how aging affects the body. Key areas include:

- Nutrition and longevity
- Sleep and recovery

See [Longevity Lifestyle Basics](/guides/longevity-lifestyle-basics) for an evidence-based overview of all three.

## Key Points

- **Aging** is the gradual decline in physiological function over time.
- **Lifespan** refers to how long someone lives.
- **Healthspan** refers to how long someone lives in good health.
- Genetics influences longevity, but lifestyle and environment strongly modify risk.
- Many longevity strategies focus on reducing disease risk and preserving function.

## Skin Aging and Longevity

Skin health is closely tied to aging biology, environmental exposure, and lifestyle. Ultraviolet radiation, collagen loss, and oxidative stress all contribute to visible and structural changes in the skin over time.

Several dermatology strategies have strong evidence for slowing skin aging and protecting long-term skin health.
