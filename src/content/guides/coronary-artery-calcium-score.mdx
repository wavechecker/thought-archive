## Intro

A coronary artery calcium (CAC) score measures calcified plaque in the coronary arteries.

It is one of the most powerful tools for refining cardiovascular risk.

---

## Key Points

- CAC directly measures atherosclerosis  
- Score of 0 → low short-term risk  
- Higher scores → higher event risk  
- Useful in borderline or uncertain cases  

---

## 🚨 Breakout: Why CAC Is Different

---

## How It Works

A CT scan detects calcium deposits in coronary arteries.

Results are expressed as a score:

- 0 → no detectable plaque  
- 1–100 → mild plaque  
- 100+ → increasing risk  

---

## When It’s Used

- Intermediate risk patients  
- Uncertain treatment decisions  
- Family history of early heart disease  

---

## Risks / Limitations

- Small radiation exposure  
- Does not detect soft plaque  
- Not needed for very low or very high risk  

---

## FAQ

**Q: Is a CAC score of 0 a guarantee?**  
A: No—but it suggests low short-term risk.

**Q: Should everyone get a CAC scan?**  
A: No—targeted use is key.
