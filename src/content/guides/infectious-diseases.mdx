# Infectious Diseases — Guide Hub

Infectious diseases are caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. They can spread between people, from animals, through food and water, or from the environment.

Most infections are mild and self-limiting. Some, however, are life-threatening — including bacterial meningitis, sepsis, tuberculosis, and severe pneumonia. Globally, infectious diseases remain among the leading causes of death, particularly in children and in low-income settings.

Vaccination, hygiene, clean water, antimicrobial stewardship, and early treatment remain the most powerful tools for prevention and control.

## Common Symptoms & When to Seek Urgent Help

### Seek emergency care immediately for:

- High fever with stiff neck or severe headache (possible meningitis)
- Rapidly spreading, painful red rash
- Signs of sepsis: confusion, very high or low temperature, rapid breathing, mottled skin
- Difficulty breathing not explained by a known condition
- Loss of consciousness or seizures
- Suspected anaphylaxis after vaccination or medication

## Measles in Focus

Measles re-emerges when vaccination rates fall below ~95% — the level needed for herd immunity.

It is not a mild illness. Measles can cause encephalitis, blindness, and death. It can also suppress immune memory for months or years.

## FAQ

**Q: What is an infectious disease?**  
A disease caused by microorganisms — bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites — that can spread between people or from the environment.

**Q: How do infections spread?**  
Through respiratory droplets, contact, food or water, blood and body fluids, insect bites, or vertical transmission from mother to child.

**Q: What is antibiotic resistance?**  
When bacteria evolve to survive antibiotic treatment, making infections harder to cure.

**Q: When should I seek emergency care?**  
For stiff neck with fever, rapidly spreading rash, breathing difficulty, confusion, signs of sepsis, or loss of consciousness.
