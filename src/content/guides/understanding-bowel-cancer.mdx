faq:
  - q: "What are the early warning signs of bowel cancer?"
    a: "Common warning signs include rectal bleeding or blood in the stool, a persistent change in bowel habits (diarrhoea, constipation, or thinner stools for more than a few weeks), unexplained weight loss, abdominal pain or discomfort, and unexplained iron-deficiency anaemia. These symptoms should always be assessed by a doctor."
  - q: "How is bowel cancer diagnosed?"
    a: "Diagnosis typically involves colonoscopy, which allows direct visualisation of the bowel lining and biopsy of any suspicious areas. CT colonography and flexible sigmoidoscopy are alternatives in some settings. Blood tests for anaemia and tumour markers may support but do not confirm the diagnosis."
  - q: "Is bowel cancer treatable?"
    a: "Yes, and outcomes are strongly related to stage at detection. Early-stage bowel cancer (confined to the bowel wall) is highly treatable and often curable with surgery alone. Later stages involving lymph nodes or distant spread require combination treatment and have lower survival rates — which is why screening to detect cancer early is important."
  - q: "What does bowel cancer screening involve?"
    a: "Most national screening programs use a faecal immunochemical test (FIT), which detects hidden blood in a stool sample. A positive result leads to colonoscopy for further investigation. Screening detects cancer and pre-cancerous polyps before symptoms develop. Eligibility, starting age, and intervals vary by country."
  - q: "Can bowel cancer be prevented?"
    a: "The risk can be reduced by maintaining a healthy weight, being physically active, eating a high-fibre diet with limited red and processed meat, not smoking, and limiting alcohol. Regular screening allows pre-cancerous polyps to be detected and removed before they become cancer."

## Intro

Bowel cancer — also called colorectal cancer — develops in the colon or rectum. It often begins as a small growth called a polyp, which may become cancerous over time.

When detected early, bowel cancer is highly treatable.

---

## Key Points

- Most bowel cancers develop slowly from polyps.
- Early stages may cause no symptoms.
- Screening significantly reduces risk.
- Treatment depends on stage.
- Lifestyle and family history influence risk.

---

## Symptoms

Common symptoms include:

- Rectal bleeding
- Persistent change in bowel habit
- Unexplained weight loss
- Iron deficiency anemia
- Ongoing abdominal pain

Symptoms do not always mean cancer — but they should be assessed.

---

## Risk Factors

- Age over 45–50
- Family history
- Inflammatory bowel disease
- Diet low in fiber, high in processed meat
- Smoking and obesity

---

## Diagnosis

Diagnosis may involve:

- FIT testing
- Colonoscopy
- Biopsy
- Imaging (CT scans)

---

## Treatment

Treatment depends on stage and may include:

- Surgery
- Chemotherapy
- Radiotherapy
- Targeted therapy

---

## Prevention

- Regular screening
- Healthy diet
- Physical activity
- Avoiding smoking
