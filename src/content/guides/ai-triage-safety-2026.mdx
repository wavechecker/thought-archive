  - q: "Is AI safe to use for triage decisions?"
    a: "Current large language models should not be used as standalone triage tools. They are not validated replacements for clinical triage systems or trained medical staff and should not be used to make urgent care decisions independently."
  - q: "What is AI triage?"
    a: "AI triage refers to using AI tools to assess symptom urgency — identifying who needs emergency care versus who can safely wait or manage at home. It is an area of active research but lacks validated clinical deployment."
  - q: "What should patients use AI health tools for?"
    a: "AI tools can be helpful for general health information, understanding conditions, and preparing questions for appointments. They should not be used to make decisions about urgent or emergency care."

    a: "A triage error occurs when a patient who needs urgent care is assessed as low-risk, or a low-risk patient is unnecessarily directed to emergency services. Both types carry patient safety implications."

In February 2026, *Nature Medicine* published a structured evaluation of ChatGPT Health’s triage recommendations.

The study reported 52% under-triage among gold-standard emergency cases in structured vignette testing.
