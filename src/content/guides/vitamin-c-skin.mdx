## Intro

Vitamin C is one of the most widely used ingredients in dermatology and skincare. It is valued for its antioxidant properties and its role in supporting collagen production.

**Vitamin C is an essential nutrient and antioxidant that helps protect skin from oxidative damage while supporting collagen synthesis.**

Because of these properties, vitamin C is commonly used in skincare products designed to improve skin tone, reduce signs of aging, and protect against environmental stress.

---

## Key Points

- Vitamin C is a powerful **antioxidant** that protects skin from oxidative damage.
- It supports **collagen synthesis**, an important structural protein in the skin.
- Topical vitamin C may help improve **skin brightness, pigmentation, and fine lines**.
- Vitamin C also helps protect skin from **environmental damage such as UV exposure and pollution**.
- Stable formulations are important because vitamin C can degrade easily.

---

## Background

Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) plays an essential role in several biological processes related to skin health.

These include:

- Collagen production
- Antioxidant protection
- Wound healing
- Regulation of skin pigmentation

Unlike many animals, humans **cannot synthesize vitamin C**, meaning it must be obtained through diet or supplements.

In dermatology, vitamin C is used both **topically** and **orally** to support skin health.

---

## How Vitamin C Works in the Skin

Vitamin C affects the skin through several mechanisms.

### Antioxidant protection

Ultraviolet radiation and pollution generate **reactive oxygen species (ROS)** in the skin. These molecules damage proteins, lipids, and DNA.

Vitamin C helps neutralize these reactive molecules, reducing oxidative stress in the skin.

### Collagen synthesis

Vitamin C is required for enzymes involved in collagen production. Without adequate vitamin C, collagen synthesis becomes impaired.

This role is why vitamin C deficiency can lead to **scurvy**, a disease characterized by weakened connective tissue.

### Pigmentation regulation

Vitamin C can help reduce excess pigmentation by interfering with **melanin production** in the skin.

This may help improve uneven skin tone and certain types of hyperpigmentation.

## Evidence for Skin Benefits

Clinical research suggests vitamin C may help improve several aspects of skin health.

Observed benefits include:

- Improved skin brightness
- Reduction in hyperpigmentation
- Increased collagen production
- Reduced oxidative skin damage

Some studies also suggest vitamin C can improve **fine wrinkles and skin texture**, particularly when combined with other skincare treatments.

However, results depend heavily on formulation stability and concentration.

---

## Topical vs Oral Vitamin C

Vitamin C can be used both through **dietary intake** and **topical skincare products**.

### Topical vitamin C

Topical vitamin C is often used in skincare serums.

Effective formulations typically contain:

- **L-ascorbic acid**
- Concentrations around **10–20%**
- Stabilizing ingredients such as vitamin E or ferulic acid

### Dietary vitamin C

Dietary vitamin C supports overall health and collagen production throughout the body.

Foods rich in vitamin C include:

- Citrus fruits
- Berries
- Peppers
- Broccoli
- Leafy greens

While oral vitamin C supports systemic health, topical vitamin C delivers higher concentrations directly to the skin.

---

## Evidence Summary

Dermatology research suggests vitamin C provides several benefits for skin health, particularly through its antioxidant activity and support for collagen production.

Topical vitamin C has been shown to:

- Reduce oxidative damage from environmental stress
- Improve uneven pigmentation
- Support collagen synthesis

However, vitamin C works best as part of a broader skincare strategy including **sun protection and other evidence-based treatments**.

## FAQ

### What does vitamin C do for the skin?

Vitamin C acts as an antioxidant, supports collagen production, and may improve skin brightness and pigmentation.

### Does vitamin C help with wrinkles?

Vitamin C may help improve fine wrinkles by supporting collagen production and protecting the skin from oxidative damage.

### Is topical vitamin C better than oral vitamin C?

Topical vitamin C delivers higher concentrations directly to the skin, while dietary vitamin C supports overall health and collagen synthesis.

### What concentration of vitamin C is best for skin?

Many dermatologists recommend formulations containing **10–20% L-ascorbic acid** for topical use.

### Can vitamin C protect against sun damage?

Vitamin C can help reduce oxidative damage from UV exposure but **does not replace sunscreen**.
