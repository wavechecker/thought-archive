## Overview

Artificial intelligence is reshaping how healthcare systems triage, diagnose, document, and decide. The efficiency gains are real — but so are the risks: automation bias, algorithmic inequity, and the gradual erosion of clinical judgement when humans defer too readily to system outputs. Understanding both sides is essential for any clinician, administrator, or policymaker working in digital health.

Applications include:

- Symptom triage  
- Clinical documentation  
- Risk prediction  
- Diagnostic assistance  
- Workflow automation  

While AI offers efficiency gains, safety, calibration, and fairness remain central concerns.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is AI replacing clinicians?**  
A: No. AI augments workflows but does not replace professional accountability.

**Q2: What is the biggest safety concern?**  
A: Escalation threshold calibration in high-risk contexts.

**Q3: Is AI regulated in healthcare?**  
A: Regulation depends on intended use and jurisdiction.

**Q4: Can AI bias be eliminated?**  
A: It can be reduced and monitored but not fully eliminated.

**Q5: What should clinicians know?**  
A: AI outputs are assistive, not authoritative.
